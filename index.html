Color Usage Guidelines

Ogre Red (#6C3333): Reserved for logo, primary CTAs like "Create Load", "Book Carrier", and brand moments. Use sparingly to maintain impact.

Dark Gray (#1A1A1A): Primary text color and secondary buttons. Ensures maximum readability across all interfaces.

Status Colors: Green for delivered/confirmed, Yellow for in-transit/at-pickup, Red for delays/errors, Blue for informational badges and links.

Medium Gray (#666666): Secondary text, labels, helper text, and disabled states.

Tone of Voice

Our Voice

We speak the language of logistics professionals. Our tone is direct and efficient, respecting that time is money in freight. We're professional but human, understanding the pressures of tight deadlines and the importance of clear communication between brokers, carriers, and shippers.

Voice Characteristics

Clear & Direct

Use logistics terminology correctly. Be specific about rates, deadlines, and requirements. No fluff.

✓ "Load booked at $2,400"
✗ "Your shipment is ready!"

Solution-Oriented

Focus on what's needed to move freight. Provide actionable next steps. Keep drivers and shippers informed.

✓ "Carrier assigned. ETA 2 hours"
✗ "Things are moving along great!"

Honest & Transparent

Be upfront about delays, pricing, and capacity. Build trust through clear communication about what's happening with loads.

✓ "Delivery delayed 3 hours - traffic"
✗ "Small hiccup, no worries!"

Status Updates

✓ Good Example

Clear, factual updates:
• "Picked up on time - Dallas, TX"
• "En route - ETA 2:00 PM MST"
• "Delivered - signed by J. Smith"
• "Delayed 90 min - traffic I-10"

✗ Bad Example

Vague or overly casual:
• "Everything's going great!"
• "Driver is on his way"
• "Should be there soon!"
• "Little delay but no biggie!"
